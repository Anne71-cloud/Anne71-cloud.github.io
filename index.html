Who I am

Students first.
Always.

I am a qualified EFL teacher based in Johannesburg, South Africa. My core belief is simple: every learner is different, and every lesson should reflect that. I do not teach to a textbook. I teach to the person, tailoring what I do to their goals, their pace, and their life.

I work with people of all ages and cultures, from young teenagers navigating their first foreign language to working adults looking to open new doors. Whatever the level, whatever the background, my purpose is to help each learner reach their own language goals in a way that feels purposeful and real.

I teach English, and I do it well. But what sets me apart is that I also speak French and Afrikaans, which means I have been a language learner myself. I know the confusion of grammar rules that do not make sense, the frustration of the right word not coming, and the confidence that builds when someone finally explains it in a way that clicks. A learner makes the best teacher, because I do not just know the language, I know what it costs to learn it, and I use that knowledge to help my students avoid the same mistakes and reach their goals faster.

Before teaching, I completed an MSc in Finance and Investment Management at the University of Salford. That experience sharpened my analytical thinking and my ability to break down complex ideas clearly, both of which make me a more effective and thoughtful teacher.

"The best teaching happens when you see the learner as a whole person, not just a language level. I put my students first because their success is the only measure that matters in the room."
